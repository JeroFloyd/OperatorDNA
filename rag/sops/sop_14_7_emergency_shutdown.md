# SOP 14.7 — Emergency Shutdown Procedure

**Revision:** 2.3
**Effective Date:** 2024-08-10
**Applicable To:** Shift supervisors, senior operators

## Purpose

Standard procedure for safe emergency shutdown of process equipment when conditions exceed safe operating limits.

## Trigger Conditions

- Pressure > 3.5 bar and rising
- Pressure > 3.0 bar with confirmed equipment failure
- Uncontrolled release of hazardous material
- Fire or explosion in adjacent area
- Upon instruction from shift supervisor

## Procedure

### Immediate Actions (within 10 seconds)

1. **Press E-Stop** — activates all safety instrumented system (SIS) interlocks
2. **Close all isolation valves** — downstream and upstream
3. **Stop all pumps** — reduce RPM to 0 immediately
4. **Energize alarm** — plant-wide evacuation signal

### Secondary Actions (within 60 seconds)

1. Verify all equipment has stopped
2. Check for any secondary hazards (leaks, fires)
3. Notify plant management
4. Begin headcount verification

### Post-Shutdown

1. Do NOT restart until investigation is complete
2. Preserve all data logs and alarm history
3. Participate in incident investigation
4. Document all actions taken

## Recovery

Restart only after:
- Incident root cause identified
- Corrective actions implemented
- Management authorization received
- Systems tested and verified
